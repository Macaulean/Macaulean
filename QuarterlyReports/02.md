# **Quarterly report**

Over this quarter, work on the Macaulean project focused on three interconnected strands: **algorithmic investigations in computational algebra**, **Lean-based formalisation and verification**, and **practical considerations for efficiency, interoperability, and future examples**. Alongside this, the team explored logistics for upcoming workshops and collaborations.

### **1. Gröbner Bases, Ideal Membership, and Performance Questions**

A recurring theme was understanding situations where the `grind` tactic fails to terminate, and identifying cases where explicit Gröbner basis computations succeed instead. This led to broader discussions about:
* methods for obtaining **smaller Gröbner bases** and **shorter certificates**;
* strategies for producing **radical membership certificates**;
* recognising the computational bottlenecks in the current approach;
* considering whether kernel-based or structural techniques (e.g., via Bhavik’s expertise) could accelerate difficult computations.

These topics were grounded in concrete examples, such as verifying that **1 lies in an ideal** defining the singular locus of $$x^2 + y^2 - 1$$. The group also discussed expanding beyond characteristic‑0 computations to explore settings over non-$$\mathbb{Q}$$ fields, motivated partly by potential speed-ups by working modulo primes and generally supporting more ground fields as coefficients.

### **2. Macaulay2 Interaction and Experimental Code**

The quarter also included experiments with AI‑generated Macaulay2 code, including a proposed simplification of a benchmark polynomial. While the output may have stemmed from an AI hallucination, it prompted useful discussions on restructuring expressions, detecting repeated patterns, and understanding how such transformations influence Gröbner basis behaviour. This fed back into thinking about certificate size and computational tractability.

### **3. Lean Formalisation: Data Structures and Verification**

Key points included:
* the design of a **custom polynomial type** that more closely mirrors Macaulay2’s internal representation, enabling clearer equality checking and, more importantly, more efficient verification of certificates;
* addressing **reducibility issues** and the use of modifiers such as `with_default`, aiming to find a good boundary for what to unfold and what to keep opaque;
* implementing and optimising **monomial sorting** and other expression‑level operations;
* exploring how to handle **integer coefficients** efficiently;
* laying out the specification for the **`ideal_membership` branch**, whose goal is to:
  * accept polynomials with `Nat` or `Int` coefficients,
  * correctly solve the ideal membership problem on modest examples,
  * postpone performance tuning and finite‑field (`ZMod p`) support to a later stage.

An additional forward‑looking discussion involved how Macaulay2 could eventually produce **proof certificates** to be verified in Lean, and what the complexity of verifying Macaulay2’s internal algorithms (e.g., polynomial multiplication) would entail.

### **4. Tooling Experiments and Automation**

The team explored the possibility of an AI‑generated `m2sos` (sum‑of‑squares) tactic, which raised broader questions about the role of AI tools in automated algebraic reasoning. This remains exploratory but aligns with longer‑term goals around certificate extraction and verification.

### **5. Logistics, Collaboration, and Planning**

The quarter also included planning for upcoming scientific interactions, including ICMS, visits to London and Warwick, and potential dates for a London event (late June, late August, or mid‑September). These discussions will support community building and feedback on Macaulean’s evolving computational and formalisation framework.
